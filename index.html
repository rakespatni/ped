Suggestion Section




The department of physical education had existed right from the inception of Calicut REC in 1962. The department had one teacher, Mr. K M K Ahamed with 2 markers to assist him. Mr. Ahamed tragically passed away due to illness while in service and other subject teachers were standing in temporarily till 2 regular Lecturers namely, M/S T.M. Abdurahiman and John Philip, took charge in 1971. Dr. A.M. Najeeb joined the department in October,1976 in place of John Philip who opted out of the institution for domestic reasons. Sri. Abdurahiman retired as Asst. Professor in July, 2002 and was replaced by Mr. Sunil M.S who joined as Lecturer in August 2005. The duo of Dr. Najeeb and Mr. Sunil continues as on date. The two regular markers in the department M/S Shanku Francis and K.Ramadasan also retired from service during 1999 and 2005 respectively. They have not been replaced yet and the department is makeshifting with temporary hands.

Objectives and Motto

Right from inception, the department of physical education has maintained its goal and motto as Total Fitness on the Campus. It still remains so in the NITC mode. Till the year 2002, as Calicut REC under University of Calicut, Physical education was a Voluntary subject with only those students who wished participated. But the cream of sports men and women participated and good teams were prepared and fielded for university, district, state and national tournaments with satisfactory results. The students then enjoyed incentives like duty leaves and sports sessional marks which certainly motivated them. How ever, we were never near our objective of total fitness due to smaller participation level. This, we tried making up with campus opens with small amount of success. The transformation to NIT Calicut has brought about a vast change with Physical education finding its rightful place in the curriculum and students flocking in great numbers to the sports infrastructure. The condition has become so overwhelming that no undergraduate student leaves the institute with out once passing through the corridors of physical education. The desired quantum of participation has been achieved and we can proudly boast that we have very nearly accomplished our objective. How ever, the participation of our teams in external tournaments has come down considerably. Academic constraints together with the lack of incentives prevent even good performers from taking part in competitive events. But on-campus activities has never been so good.
